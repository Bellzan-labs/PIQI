# Auto — competitor audit

## Competitors surveyed

### [Maaco](https://www.maaco.com)
- **Positioning:** Auto body shop, car paint shop, and collision repair services.
- **Headline(s):** "Make the Most of Your Refund"; "Auto Body Painting"; "Headlight Restoration"; "Light Collision Repair."
- **Services:** Auto painting, headlight restoration, collision repair, fleet services, special services.
- **Tone:** Accessible, solution-focused, everyday-driver language — affordability foregrounded.
- **Trust signals:** Certified technicians, national warranty policies, Driven Brands affiliation, franchise network scale.
- **Pricing:** Promotional/seasonal — "30% off paint packages" during tax season; value framing.
- **FAQ topics:** Not on the homepage; accessible via dedicated link.
- **CTA language:** "FIND YOUR MAACO"; "Book An Appointment"; "Learn More."
- **Imagery:** Service category cards with vehicle condition focus; promotional banners.
- **Notable copy:** "Turn that refund check into an opportunity to finally fix those dents, fading paint, or everyday wear that continue to drag down your vehicle's appearance."

## Patterns across competitors
- Panel-beating and tyre retail sit in a local-trust market — SA operators lead with years, process diagrams, and "number of cars repaired," not brand voice.
- Pricing splits cleanly: **retail parts/tyres transparent**, **repair services opaque**.
- "WhatsApp us" is normalised as a first-contact CTA in SA.
- Process transparency (Auto Magic's 24-step, Maaco's stepped repair) substitutes for pricing transparency — clients want to know what happens, not what it costs.
- Insurance-literacy language appears on panel beaters (no-claim bonus warnings, claim handling) and nowhere else.

## Distinctive moves worth borrowing
- Auto Magic's "Magic 6" and 24-step repair diagram — visible process beats unspecified promises.
- Maaco's seasonal "tax refund" hook — a concrete reason to act now, grounded in a familiar event.
- Midas's "everything your car needs under one roof" — simple consolidation line for a multi-service shop.
- BMW's configurator-style pricing exposure — even partial pricing transparency widens trust.

## Gaps PIQI could own
- No competitor combines **panel beating** and **tyre sales** under one brand; PIQI's pairing is unusual and worth naming.
- No one explains **insurance vs. cash** jobs clearly at the top of the page — it's the first question a customer has.
- Timing estimates ("your car back in X days") are universally absent.
- Fleet and corporate account handling is barely surfaced in the SMB panel-beating category.

## Tone register for this vertical
Straight, service-counter, no marketing fluff. Speak to the customer at the worst moment — after an accident, after a blow-out. Name the worry (insurance claim, no-claim bonus, courtesy car), then resolve it. Local-trust cues first, brand cues second.

## Recommendations for PIQI's auto page

### Content we can add now
- A clear split of the two lanes: **panel beating / body repair** and **tyre sales / fitment** — with a one-liner for when each applies.
- An "insurance claim vs. cash job" block stating plainly what PIQI handles either way.
- A "what happens when you bring your car in" process sequence (assessment, quote, approval, repair, collection).
- Explicit "WhatsApp us a photo of the damage for an initial view" CTA.
- Fleet/corporate account line for businesses with multiple vehicles.

### Content that needs client input first
- Typical turnaround days by job type.
- Cars-repaired volume or years-in-business stat.
- Insurance panel affiliations and accreditations (SAMBRA, etc.).
- Named fleet clients.
- Warranty terms on paint and panel work.

### FAQ topic additions
- Will fixing this through insurance affect my no-claim bonus?
- Do you give cash-job discounts for small dents and scratches?
- Is there a warranty on paint work?
- Can I drop off the car and collect it, or do I need to wait for assessment?
- Do you handle courtesy car or loan vehicle arrangements?
- How long is a typical tyre change vs. a full panel beat?
