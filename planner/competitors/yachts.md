## Patterns across competitors
- Big international operators are **destination-led**, not vessel-led — the hero sells the place first, the boat second.
- Large fleets expose pricing, solo operators don't — and solo operators lean on named testimonials instead.
- "Crewed / skippered / bareboat" is the universal decision frame for qualified buyers.
- Tone across the category is aspirational-but-warm; "Ahoy," "picture yourself," "your dream vacation" are the defaults.
- Gift cards, discounts, and loyalty programs appear on mid-market operators; premium-positioning operators avoid them.

## Distinctive moves worth borrowing
- The Moorings' strike-through pricing on real calendar dates — trust through transparency.
- Waterfront Charters' mix of scheduled sailings and private charter on one page — signals choice without overwhelm.
- Atlantis Yacht Charters' named client logos for corporate events — proof B2B is served.
- Catamaran Charter's explicit trip-planning help ("Talk to an expert") as a first-step CTA before the booking engine.

## Gaps PIQI could own
- None of the operators surveyed emphasise **self-catering on a catamaran** as a distinct, lower-friction offer — PIQI's format is unusually specific.
- SA competitors are overwhelmingly scheduled-cruise operators; a dedicated private self-catering catamaran experience is a gap.
- No one publishes a simple "what the four cabins look like and who sleeps where" guide — group composition is exactly the anxiety point.
- Provisioning honesty — what's stocked, what you bring — is barely discussed anywhere in plain language.

## Tone register for this vertical
Calm, specific, skipper-next-to-you. Less "dream vacation," more "here is what day one actually looks like." Honest about weather, cabin layout, and what a self-catering trip requires. Trust comes from detail, not adjectives.

## Recommendations for PIQI's yachts page

### Content we can add now
- A cabin-layout explanation with group composition examples (two couples, a family of eight, etc.).
- A self-catering vs. fully-crewed comparison block — who should choose which.
- A "your first day on board" paragraph — arrival, briefing, provisioning handover, first sail.
- Clear articulation of what PIQI provides vs. what guests bring.
- Seasonality guidance (best months, wind conditions, typical routes).

### Content that needs client input first
- Real cabin photography and deck plans.
- Sample itineraries with place names.
- Day-rate / week-rate pricing bands.
- Named charter references or testimonials.
- Skipper availability and credentials.

### FAQ topic additions
- How does self-catering provisioning actually work?
- Can I add a skipper, and what does that cost?
- What's the minimum sailing experience needed to bareboat?
- What happens if weather forces a route change?
- Pets on board?
- Insurance and deposit expectations?
