## Patterns across competitors
- Enterprise coaching platforms all lead with **metrics** (ROI, behaviour change, efficiency uplift); solo coaches lead with **named clients**.
- "Request a demo" dominates as CTA at enterprise scale; "book an appointment" or "newsletter" dominates at individual-practitioner scale.
- Neuroscience and AI are the two credibility frames of the moment; everyone cites both.
- Free content (courses, articles, research) is used as a top-of-funnel trust builder even by premium operators.
- Certifications (ICF, EMCC) and compliance (GDPR, ISO) are listed prominently — signals that enterprise buyers expect.

## Distinctive moves worth borrowing
- CoachHub's "top 5% of applicants" coach-quality stat — a vivid proof-of-rigour.
- NLI's "change in weeks, not years" headline — reframes the category's biggest objection (coaching is slow).
- Marshall Goldsmith's freemium generosity — free courses as the top rung of the ladder.
- BetterUp's layered product map (Lead/Manage/Grow/Ready) — clarity on who gets coached for what.

## Gaps PIQI could own
- SA-grounded coaching that names **local operational context** (load-shedding, hybrid work realities, expat-leader transitions) is thinly served by these global players.
- None pair **leadership**, **communication**, and **work-life balance** in a single offer — most pick one track. PIQI's three-lane framing is differentiated.
- Individual clients (not enterprise L&D buyers) are almost invisible on enterprise-platform pages — PIQI can address the person and the sponsor on one page.
- Outcomes described in **human terms** (harder conversations handled, clearer weeks) instead of in % ROI is a tone gap worth taking.

## Tone register for this vertical
Warm, clinical enough to be credible, not therapeutic-soft. Name the behavioural change in plain words: "harder conversations, said earlier." Avoid transformation-speak. Honest about what coaching is and isn't — including what takes weeks vs. months. Sound like a senior practitioner, not a brochure.

## Recommendations for PIQI's coaching page

### Content we can add now
- A three-lane page: **leadership**, **communication**, **work-life balance** — each with a one-sentence "who this is for" and a one-sentence "what changes."
- A "first session, what happens" block (intake, objectives, cadence) — reduces mystery.
- A short note on individual vs. sponsor-paid engagements (who sets goals, who sees progress).
- Outcome descriptions in human terms ("the conversation you've been avoiding for six weeks happens next Tuesday") before any metric claims.

### Content that needs client input first
- Coach bios, credentials, ICF / EMCC accreditations.
- Typical engagement length and session cadence.
- Named client or sector references.
- Pricing or engagement-size bands.
- Any measurable outcomes from past engagements.

### FAQ topic additions
- How many sessions does a typical engagement last, and over what period?
- Is coaching confidential when my employer is paying?
- What's the difference between coaching, mentoring, and therapy in your work?
- Do you coach individuals, teams, or both?
- How do you measure progress without making it feel like a performance review?
