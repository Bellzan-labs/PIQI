## Patterns across competitors
- Bespoke and bridal houses rarely publish prices; they frame spend as "investment."
- Booking an appointment is the default CTA — not "buy" or "enquire."
- Heritage claims (years, locations, royal warrants) do heavy lifting where client lists can't.
- Designer-led voice — named founder, personal language — dominates bridal; ready-to-wear leans brand-anonymous and visual-only.
- FAQs cluster around appointments, lead times, fabric/fitting logistics, and international service.

## Distinctive moves worth borrowing
- Casey Jeanne's "the designer / the appointment / the investment" three-block map — answers the three questions a bespoke client actually asks.
- Huntsman's gift-guide price rungs (£250, £500) — a way to signal accessibility without discounting the core craft.
- Henry Poole's swatch library as a lead magnet — a real reason to get in touch before committing.
- Elbeth Gillis's "slow fashion, in-house team" framing — craft provenance made concrete.

## Gaps PIQI could own
- Nobody combines **bespoke wedding/matric**, **uniforms**, and **branded wear** under one roof — PIQI's range is unusual and defensible.
- SA bespoke/bridal sites speak only to brides; PIQI can address **corporate uniform buyers** and **school event coordinators** in the same house without diluting the bridal tone.
- Lead-time transparency (how many weeks from fitting to final fitting) is rarely stated up front.
- Fabric-origin and make-location storytelling is under-used in SA despite being a strong differentiator.

## Tone register for this vertical
Warm, craft-led, confident about timelines and fabric. Less flowery than couture bridal; more personal than ready-to-wear. Speaks to both the nervous matric-dance parent and the procurement officer buying 300 branded polos — without changing voice. Designer as named craftsperson, not an abstract brand.

## Recommendations for PIQI's fashion page

### Content we can add now
- A three-lane split: **bespoke occasion** (weddings, matric), **uniforms**, **branded wear** — each with its own mini-flow so buyers self-route.
- Lead-time honesty per lane (e.g. "typical bespoke gown: X fittings across Y weeks") stated generically.
- A "first appointment, what to expect" block, written as reassurance for nervous first-time bespoke clients.
- Fabric-and-make provenance paragraph (where things are made, by whom, at what pace).

### Content that needs client input first
- Actual lead-time numbers per service lane.
- Named brides, matric clients, or corporate uniform accounts.
- Photography of real PIQI-made work (bridal, uniforms, branded).
- Any press or award signals for Downtown Fashion.

### FAQ topic additions
- How many fittings does a bespoke gown need, and over what period?
- Can a bridal client bring their own fabric or reference images?
- Minimum order quantity for uniforms and branded wear?
- Do you handle sizing runs for staff across multiple sites?
- How are alterations handled after final delivery?
