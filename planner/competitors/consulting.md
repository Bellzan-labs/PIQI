## Patterns across competitors
- Positioning lines are short, active, future-facing ("Shape the future," "Let there be change," "Champion the bold").
- Nobody publishes pricing; value is expressed as outcomes, recognitions, or named clients.
- Heavy use of research/insight content as primary lead magnet — thought leadership substitutes for both FAQs and case studies.
- Calls to action are mostly "Read more / Get in touch / Explore" rather than direct commercial asks.
- Trust leans on scale (offices, years, certifications) and named Fortune-tier clients.

## Distinctive moves worth borrowing
- Bain's quantified outcome stats ("$300M saved") — specific numbers sharpen advisory promises.
- Accenture's stakeholder stat wall (years on ethics list, workplace ranks) — framing credibility around *how* they work, not *whom*.
- EY's single question headline ("Will you shape the future or be shaped by it?") — invites the reader into a decision.
- KPMG's "services that make the difference" phrasing — frames services as the differentiator, not the bolt-on.

## Gaps PIQI could own
- No Big Four site foregrounds **commercial project delivery at mid-market scale** (FEL, procurement gates, contract forms). PIQI's engagement is specific and practical where theirs is abstract.
- None emphasise **project-stage clarity** (FEL1–FEL4) up front — PIQI can treat that as a navigable map.
- Big players speak at C-suite altitude. A South African operator-to-operator tone (procurement managers, project leads) is wide open.
- Zero pricing transparency anywhere — even "typical engagement shape" language would stand out.

## Tone register for this vertical
Plainspoken, senior, operational. Fewer adjectives than the Big Four. Speak as a practitioner who has sat in contract reviews and procurement committees. Avoid transformation cliches; lead with the artefacts (FEL packages, procurement strategies, contract forms) and what happens at each gate.

## Recommendations for PIQI's consulting page

- A "what each FEL stage actually produces" block — deliverables, decisions, typical duration — written as an operator would.
