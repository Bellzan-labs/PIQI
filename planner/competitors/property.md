# Property — competitor audit

## Competitors surveyed

### [Trafalgar](https://www.trafalgar.co.za)
- **Positioning:** "One-stop Property Solutions" — comprehensive property management and real estate across South Africa.
- **Headline(s):** Property Letting Services; Sectional Title & HOA Management; Commercial Property Management.
- **Services:** Vacancy marketing, landlord and tenant services, sales agents, valuations, full-service management, commercial landlord services, body corporate loans, levy finance.
- **Tone:** Professional, accessible, solution-oriented. Corporate credibility in customer-friendly language.
- **Trust signals:** 50-year property management track record; PPRA, NAMA and Council for Debt Collectors registration; 7 branches; free consultations.
- **Pricing:** Not disclosed; free assessments used to qualify leads.
- **FAQ topics:** Not a dedicated block on the homepage; information surfaces in service cards.
- **CTA language:** "Use us Now!"; "Book your FREE 15 minute community scheme consultation"; "Enroll Now!"
- **Imagery:** Service category cards with icons, branch photos, resource thumbnails.

### [Just Property](https://just.property/)
- **Positioning:** "Just Property exists to help our clients achieve wealth and security through property sales and rentals."
- **Headline(s):** "Why Sell With Us"; "Why Let With Us."
- **Services:** Residential, commercial, industrial, retail, mixed-use, agricultural, smallholdings, vacant land, holiday letting, student accommodation, property management.
- **Tone:** Approachable, professional, reassuring — peace of mind and accessibility.
- **Trust signals:** 25+ years; "tens of thousands of clients helped"; "#1 rental agency in SA"; 100+ offices; PPRA, FASA, Better Bond affiliations.
- **Pricing:** Not disclosed; free valuations and CMA.
- **FAQ topics:** Not present on the page surveyed.
- **CTA language:** "Contact us today for a no-obligation comparative market analysis"; "Sell/Let With Us."
- **Imagery:** Property-focused photography, leadership visuals, minimal corporate branding.
- **Notable copy:** "Buying, renting, selling, or letting property is a big deal, which is why it's so important to place your trust in a brand that can give you the peace of mind you deserve."

### [Pam Golding Properties](https://www.pamgolding.co.za)

- **Headline(s):** "Find your next home, apartment or property"; "Luxury Collection"; "Lifestyle Living."

- **Notable copy:** "Live how you want to, where you want to."

### [Seeff Property Group](https://www.seeff.com)

- **FAQ topics:** Not present.

- **Imagery:** Rotating branded hero, lifestyle property photography.

## Patterns across competitors
- Every agency leads with a decades-long heritage number (25, 50, 60 years).
- Sales and letting dominate; property **maintenance** and **construction support** are barely mentioned as services in their own right.
- Pricing is only visible on listings, never on the service.
- Regulatory credentials (PPRA, NAMA, FASA, Council for Debt Collectors) are table stakes for trust.
- Imagery is property-first — buildings and lifestyle — rather than people doing the work.

## Distinctive moves worth borrowing
- Trafalgar's "FREE 15 minute community scheme consultation" — a tiny, concrete first step that lowers friction.
- Seeff's "R1B+ monthly sales / 600 new beginnings" stat wall — quantity made vivid.
- Just Property's emotional frame ("a big deal, peace of mind") — permission to pick a brand, not a property.
- Pam Golding's mix of transparent starting prices on developments with private enquiry on individual listings.

## Gaps PIQI could own
- None of these competitors foreground **maintenance** as a standalone commercial service — they treat it as an embedded part of rental management. PIQI can own it.
- None integrate **construction support** with letting and maintenance under one group. PIQI's vertical stack is unusually joined-up.
- No one addresses landlords with small-to-medium portfolios as a distinct audience — the market is polarised between DIY landlords and full-service corporates.
- Fast-response, same-contact service is under-claimed. Agency sites feel branch-routed, not contact-routed.

## Tone register for this vertical
Calm, capable, local. Short sentences. Names the awkward stuff landlords worry about (vacancies, late payment, maintenance emergencies, compliance) and answers them plainly. Avoid luxury lifestyle cliches unless listing high-end stock; lead with responsiveness and accountability.

## Recommendations for PIQI's property page

### Content we can add now
- A "what we do when something breaks at 10pm" block — the service promise around response time and single point of contact.
- A three-lane framing: **letting**, **maintenance**, **construction support** — written as parallel services, not a bundle.
- A short explanation of how PIQI supports landlords vs. supports body corporates (implied audience routing, even without a selector).
- Compliance and regulatory language stated plainly (what PIQI handles vs. what the landlord must).

### Content that needs client input first
- Specific response-time SLAs.
- Number of units under management or projects supported.
- Named developments, landlords, or case outcomes.

### FAQ topic additions
- What counts as an emergency maintenance call-out, and what does the landlord pay for?
- Do you handle compliance (COC, beetle, electrical, gas) on our behalf?
- How do you vet tenants and how quickly can you fill a vacancy?
- Can you quote on a small renovation while you manage the letting?
- What reporting do landlords get, and how often?
- How do you coordinate with the body corporate on sectional title stock?
